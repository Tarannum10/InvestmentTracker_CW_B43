Government & Economic Policies

As of March 28, 2025, India has implemented several significant government and economic policies aimed at bolstering domestic industries, enhancing international trade relations, and managing fiscal health.

Boost to Electronics Manufacturing:The Union Cabinet has approved a ₹22,919 crore (approximately $2.7 billion) Production -Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme to strengthen domestic manufacturing of electronic components. This initiative targets increased production in sectors such as telecommunications, automotive, and energy, and is anticipated to create around 92,000 direct jobs. The scheme offers employment-linked incentives to manufacturers, aligning with India's goal to become a global electronics manufacturing hub.

Trade Policy Adjustments:In efforts to improve trade relations with the United States and mitigate potential tariff impacts, India has undertaken several measures:

  • Elimination of Digital Advertising Tax: India has repealed the 6% "Google tax" on online advertisements, benefiting major U.S. tech companies like Google, Meta, and Amazon. This move aims to level the playing field for Indian companies and smooth trade negotiations with the U.S.
  • Consideration of Import Tax Removal on U.S. LNG: The government is contemplating removing import taxes on U.S. liquefied natural gas (LNG) to boost imports and address the trade surplus with the U.S., which stood at $45.4 billion last year. This initiative seeks to make U.S. LNG more competitively priced and aligns with India's commitment to increase energy purchases from the U.S.
  • Additional Measures: India plans to increase energy and defense purchases from the U.S., reduce tariffs on products like bourbon whiskey and luxury cars, and facilitate collaborations with companies such as Elon Musk's Starlink for satellite broadband services. These steps are part of ongoing negotiations to finalize a trade agreement and resolve tariff disputes.

Fiscal Management:India's fiscal deficit for the April-February period reached ₹13.47 trillion (approximately $157.62 billion), accounting for 85.8% of the annual target. Net tax receipts during this period were ₹20.16 trillion, representing 78.8% of the yearly goal. Government expenditure totaled ₹38.93 trillion, with capital expenditure at ₹8.12 trillion. The government aims to reduce the fiscal deficit to 4.4% of GDP by 2025-26 and plans to shift its fiscal policy benchmark to the debt-to-GDP ratio, targeting a reduction to 50% by March 2031 from the current 57%.

Economic Growth:The Indian economy expanded by 6.2% in the October-December quarter, driven by increased government and consumer spending. The government anticipates further acceleration in growth during the current quarter, reflecting resilience amid global economic uncertainties.

These policy measures underscore India's commitment to fostering economic growth, enhancing trade relationships, and maintaining fiscal discipline in a dynamic global environment.

Banking & Finance News

As of March 28, 2025, India's banking and finance sector is experiencing notable developments across various domains, including digital payments, liquidity management, market performance, and regulatory actions.

Digital Payments Expansion

India is actively promoting its Unified Payments Interface (UPI) system for international transactions to compete with global networks like Visa and Mastercard. The government is lobbying the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) to ease compliance requirements for cross-border payments made through UPI. This initiative aims to facilitate UPI usage for Indians traveling abroad and enhance competition in the international payments market. Currently, India has agreements with seven countries, including France and Singapore, to accept UPI payments.

Liquidity Management Measures

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is considering reintroducing a fixed daily funding scheme to help banks manage liquidity more effectively amid significant fluctuations. These fluctuations have been influenced by the RBI's substantial forex interventions and unpredictable government spending patterns. Senior bankers and central bank officials are scheduled to meet next week to discuss a revised liquidity management framework, which could impact interest rates, bank lending, and overall economic growth.

Market Performance and Financial Stocks

In March, India's financial sector stocks led a recovery in the Nifty 50 index following a historic decline, positioning the market for a strong fiscal year 2026. Anticipated interest rate cuts by the RBI, credit growth, and the return of foreign capital inflows have bolstered confidence in financial stocks. Analysts project a 12-13% credit growth for the banking sector in 2026, driven by demand from services and retail segments.

Government Borrowing Plans

The Indian government plans to raise ₹8 trillion (approximately $93.34 billion) through bond sales from April to September, accounting for 54% of its estimated gross borrowing for the upcoming fiscal year. The target fiscal deficit for 2025-26 is set at 4.4% of GDP, down from the current year's target of 4.8%. Additionally, the government intends to sell ₹100 billion worth of green bonds in the next six months, following previous sales of ₹200 billion.

Banking Sector Initiatives

  • Axis Bank and J.P. Morgan Collaboration: Axis Bank, in partnership with J.P. Morgan, has launched real-time U.S. dollar payment capabilities for commercial clients in India. This initiative, facilitated by J.P. Morgan's blockchain and digital assets unit, Kinexys, marks the first instance where an Indian company can make or receive dollar payments at any time.
  • Jio Financial Services Investment: Jio Financial Services plans to invest ₹1,000 crore in Jio Finance, aiming to expand its footprint in the financial services sector.

Regulatory Actions

The Economic Offences Wing of the Mumbai Police has arrested multiple individuals, including former executives, in connection with the alleged misappropriation of ₹122 crore from New India Cooperative Bank. The Reserve Bank of India has imposed withdrawal restrictions and superseded the bank's board for 12 months due to irregular lending practices.

These developments underscore the dynamic nature of India's banking and finance sector, highlighting ongoing efforts to enhance digital payment systems, manage liquidity, strengthen market performance, and enforce regulatory compliance.

Commodities & Gold Prices

As of March 28, 2025, the commodities market in India is experiencing notable fluctuations across various sectors, including precious metals, agriculture, and industrial materials.

Gold Prices and Market Dynamics

Gold prices have surged to unprecedented levels, with domestic rates in India reaching a record high of ₹89,796 per 10 grams earlier this month, marking a nearly 15% increase year-to-date.This sharp rise has led to subdued demand in the physical gold market, as consumers and jewelers grapple with elevated costs. Many jewelers are currently focused on closing their financial year accounts, further dampening purchasing activity. Consequently, Indian dealers have been offering discounts of up to $33 per ounce over official domestic prices, slightly lower than the previous week's $41 discount.


The high gold prices have also prompted a significant influx of old jewelry and coins into the market, as individuals seek to capitalize on the favorable rates. This trend is evident across major markets in Asia and the Middle East, where jewelers report increased selling back of gold items.

Agricultural Commodities

  • Rice:India, the world's largest rice exporter, has lifted its remaining restrictions on rice exports in a bid to double agricultural shipments by 2030 and stimulate economic growth. This policy shift has led to a significant decrease in global rice prices, impacting producers in countries like Pakistan, Thailand, and Vietnam, who had temporarily benefited from India's earlier export limitations. The move is expected to benefit markets in Africa and East Asia by providing access to more affordable rice supplies.
  • Steel:The imposition of U.S. tariffs on steel and aluminum imports is anticipated to reshape global trade flows, potentially leading to an oversupply in the Indian market. S&P Global forecasts a price correction of approximately ₹3,000 per ton for Indian steel. Domestic steelmakers are already contending with increased imports from countries such as China, South Korea, and Japan, which have been offering steel at discounted rates.

Market Outlook

Looking ahead, major financial institutions project that gold's bullish trend will persist into 2025, driven by factors such as increased inflows into exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and anticipated interest rate cuts by central banks, including the U.S. Federal Reserve. Goldman Sachs, for instance, forecasts that gold prices could reach $2,900 per ounce by early 2025, supported by sustained central bank purchases and renewed investor interest.

In summary, India's commodities market is navigating a complex landscape influenced by policy changes, global trade dynamics, and fluctuating demand. Stakeholders are advised to monitor these developments closely to make informed decisions in this evolving environment.

Cryptocurrency & Digital Assets

As of March 28, 2025, India's cryptocurrency and digital assets landscape is experiencing significant developments across frameworks, market dynamics, and technological advancements.

Regulatory Developments

The Indian government has proposed new provisions under the Income Tax Bill, 2025, aimed at enhancing the tracking and investigation of digital assets. Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman highlighted the necessity of these measures to address the challenges posed by digital transactions, emphasizing their role in combating tax evasion and ensuring compliance.

In a notable shift, India's stance on cryptocurrencies appears to be evolving, influenced by international trends and policies. The administration is recalibrating its approach, potentially aligning more closely with global standards.

Market Dynamics

Cryptocurrency trading has seen a significant uptick in India's smaller cities, driven by individuals seeking alternative income sources amid economic challenges. Trading volumes for major cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum have doubled in recent months, particularly in non-metro areas such as Nagpur, Jaipur, and Lucknow. This surge persists despite a 30% tax on crypto gains and regulatory uncertainties.

The futures trading segment is also gaining traction, offering traders leverage and potential tax efficiencies. This shift reflects a maturing market as participants seek sophisticated trading instruments.

Exchange Activities

Coinbase, a leading U.S.-based cryptocurrency exchange, has registered with India's Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU), signaling its intent to re-enter the Indian market. This move underscores a broader trend of major crypto exchanges eyeing India as a significant market opportunity, especially as regulatory attitudes appear to be softening.

Security Concerns

The cryptocurrency sector continues to grapple with security challenges. In a notable incident, Chirag Tomar was sentenced to five years in prison for orchestrating a phishing scheme that involved a fake Coinbase website, resulting in the theft of $20 million from customers. This case highlights the critical need for heightened vigilance and robust security measures within the crypto community.

Overall, India's cryptocurrency and digital assets ecosystem is at a pivotal juncture, marked by evolving regulations, dynamic market participation, and ongoing security considerations. Stakeholders are advised to stay informed and exercise due diligence as the landscape continues to develop.

Real Estate & REITs

As of March 28, 2025, India's real estate sector is experiencing significant growth and transformation, marked by robust market performance, regulatory developments, and notable corporate activities.

Market Growth and Projections

India's real estate industry is projected to expand from ₹40 lakh crore in 2023 to ₹83 lakh crore by 2030, driven by urbanization, rising incomes, and government initiatives like 'Housing for All' and the Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA). The sector's contribution to GDP is expected to increase from 7.3% in 2023 to 13% by 2030. Additionally, the interior fit-out market, integral to commercial real estate, is anticipated to grow from ₹27,700 crore in 2023 to ₹80,500 crore by 2030, reflecting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 16.5%.

Residential Market Trends

Home prices in India are forecasted to rise by 6.5% in 2025 and 6.0% in 2026, outpacing the previous year's 4.0% increase. Urban rental costs are also expected to climb between 7.0% and 10.0%, surpassing the anticipated consumer inflation rate of approximately 4.3% to 4.4% over the next two fiscal years. This trend may pose affordability challenges, particularly for first-time homebuyers.

Government Initiatives and Policy Changes

The Indian government has implemented tax relief measures to boost middle-class spending power, including income tax cuts in the 2025-26 budget. These initiatives have positively impacted the real estate sector, with the Nifty Realty Index rising by 3.3%. Companies such as Prestige Estates, DLF, and Sobha have experienced notable gains. Experts suggest that these changes will enhance disposable income, stimulate demand for affordable housing, and support residential investments, particularly in smaller cities.

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs)

India's REIT market continues to evolve, offering investors opportunities for steady income and capital appreciation. Currently, four REITs are listed on Indian stock exchanges: Embassy REIT, Mindspace REIT, Brookfield India Trust, and Nexus Select Trust. Collectively, these REITs distributed ₹4,259 crore in the first nine months of FY25, marking a 15% year-on-year growth.

In a significant development, Knowledge Realty Trust, a joint venture between Sattva Group and Blackstone, has filed for India's largest REIT initial public offering (IPO) worth ₹6,200 crore. The trust owns 48 million square feet across six cities, positioning it as the most geographically diverse office REIT in India.

Notable Corporate Developments

  • DLF's Luxury Project: DLF, India's largest real estate developer, announced a $4 billion ultra-luxury project near New Delhi, expected to take over four years to complete. Targeting affluent local and non-resident Indian buyers, the development features 420 units priced at $8 million each. Amenities include cascading lakes, cinemas, on-call chefs, and indoor sports facilities. Sales for April-December 2024 reached $2.2 billion, surpassing DLF’s full fiscal-year projection.
  • Godrej Properties Acquisition: Godrej Properties has acquired a 6.54-acre land parcel in Navi Mumbai for ₹717 crore, aiming to develop a residential project. This strategic move aligns with the company's expansion plans in key urban centers.

Environmental and Legal Challenges

The Adani Group is facing multiple legal challenges related to the environmental impact of its projects. These include allegations of commencing work on a $2 billion power plant in Uttar Pradesh without environmental clearance and disputes over land use for a luxury residential project in Mumbai's Bandra area. Additionally, the group's Dharavi slum redevelopment project is contested due to concerns about redeveloping ecologically sensitive land. These legal proceedings may affect the timelines and execution of Adani's projects.

Conclusion

India's real estate sector is poised for substantial growth, driven by favorable government policies, increasing urbanization, and rising investor interest in REITs. However, challenges related to affordability, environmental concerns, and legal disputes may influence the trajectory of the market. Stakeholders are advised to monitor these developments closely to navigate the evolving landscape effectively.

Personal Finance & Investment Tips

As of March 29, 2025, here are the latest updates and insights in personal finance and investment for individuals in India:

Government Schemes and Interest Rates

  • Small Savings Schemes: The government has maintained the interest rates for Public Provident Fund (PPF) and National Savings Certificate (NSC) for the April-June quarter. PPF continues to offer an annual interest rate of 7.1%, while NSC offers 6.8%.

Banking and ATM Charges

  • ATM Withdrawal Fees: Effective May 1, ATM charges have increased by ₹2, raising the fee to ₹23 per transaction beyond the free monthly usage limit. Account holders should plan their cash withdrawals accordingly to minimize additional charges.

Taxation and Employee Benefits

  • Dearness Allowance (DA) Hike: The Union Cabinet has approved a 2% increase in Dearness Allowance for government employees, enhancing their monthly income and aiding in offsetting inflationary pressures.

Investment Options and Strategies

  • Low-Risk Investment Avenues:
    • Fixed Deposits (FDs): Consistently regarded as one of the safest investment options, FDs offer assured returns over a fixed tenure, making them suitable for conservative investors.
    • Public Provident Fund (PPF): With a 15-year tenure, PPF not only provides tax-free returns but also aids in long-term wealth accumulation.
  • Medium to High-Risk Investment Avenues:
    • Mutual Funds: Investing in equity or debt mutual funds can offer higher returns, though they come with associated market risks. It's advisable to align mutual fund investments with your financial goals and risk tolerance.
    • Stocks: Direct equity investments have the potential for substantial returns but require a thorough understanding of the stock market and a higher risk appetite.

Recent Market Developments

  • Stock Market Performance: The Nifty 50 index experienced a 6.3% rise in March, marking its best performance in 15 months and aiding a recovery from a historic five-month losing streak. This surge contributed to a 5.34% gain for the fiscal year ending in March 2025.
  • Foreign Investment in Bonds: Foreign ownership of Indian government bonds has surpassed ₹3 trillion, driven by expectations of an interest rate cut in April. This influx indicates growing confidence in India's debt market.

Investment Tips for Beginners

  • Start Early: Commencing your investment journey early allows the power of compounding to work in your favor, leading to substantial wealth accumulation over time.
  • Set Clear Goals: Define your financial objectives, such as buying a house, funding education, or retirement planning, to choose suitable investment avenues.
  • Diversify Portfolio: Spreading investments across various asset classes—equities, debt, real estate—can mitigate risks and enhance potential returns.
  • Assess Risk Tolerance: Understanding your ability to endure market fluctuations is crucial in selecting appropriate investment instruments.
  • Stay Informed: Regularly update yourself on financial news and market trends to make informed investment decisions.
